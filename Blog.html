Autism Spectrum Disorder

The Child Gaze Analysis Project aims to explore and understand the visual attention patterns of children in various educational settings. By leveraging advanced eye-tracking technology and machine learning algorithms, the project seeks to analyze how children engage with educational materials, such as books, digital media, and instructional aids. Through comprehensive data collection and analysis, the project aims to identify key factors influencing children's attention, including content complexity, visual design, and cognitive load. Insights from this research will inform the development of more effective educational materials and instructional strategies tailored to children's unique learning needs, ultimately enhancing educational outcomes and fostering a deeper understanding of child development.

Intrusion Detection

The Intrusion Detection Project endeavors to enhance network security by developing advanced intrusion detection systems (IDS). Leveraging machine learning algorithms and anomaly detection techniques, the project aims to detect and mitigate malicious activities within computer networks in real-time. By analyzing network traffic patterns, system logs, and user behaviors, the IDS identifies suspicious activities indicative of potential cyber threats, such as unauthorized access, malware infections, and denial-of-service attacks. Through continuous monitoring and automated response mechanisms, the project aims to strengthen the resilience of network infrastructures against evolving cyber threats, safeguarding critical assets and preserving data integrity.
